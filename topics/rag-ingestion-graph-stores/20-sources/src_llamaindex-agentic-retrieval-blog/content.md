Excerpt from LlamaIndex blog post by Sourabh Desai (2025-05-29):

> In this basic approach [Naive RAG], document chunks are stored in a vector database, and query embeddings are matched with the `k` most similar chunk embeddings. [Agentic retrieval, by contrast, uses] LLM-based classification to decide which sub-index (or indices) are relevant, [employing] agents at multiple layers to intelligently route queries. [...] For these agents to operate effectively and autonomously, they need precise and relevant context at their fingertips.
